# Customer Discovery Questions

## Project Title
Pixic: Campus Digital Art Community Platform

## Target Respondent
Digital Visual Artists (Student Illustrators, Designers, Hobbyists)

## Consent and Context
1. May we ask you a few questions for our ICT111 course project?
2. Your participation is voluntary. We will not collect unnecessary personal data. Is it okay if we take notes?

## Recent Experience
3. Tell us about the last time you shared your digital artwork with other students on campus.
4. What were you trying to achieve at that time (e.g., getting constructive critiques, finding collaborators, showing off a finished piece)?
5. What happened from start to finish (how did you share it, and what kind of engagement did you get)?

## Current Workaround
6. How do you usually find, follow, or interact with other digital artists on campus now?
7. What tools, apps, groups (like Telegram/Line chats, Instagram, Google Drive folders) do you currently use to show your portfolio?
8. What is difficult, slow, confusing, or frustrating about these methods (e.g., your art getting buried, low-quality feedback, lack of organization)?

## Pain Severity and Consequence
9. How often do you feel limited by the lack of a dedicated campus art community or structured gallery space?
10. How serious is this problem on a scale of 1–5? Why?
11. What happens if this problem is not solved (e.g., loss of motivation, inability to find local art partners)?

## Closing
12. Is there anything else we should understand about how campus artists share and discuss their works?
13. May we use your anonymized response as part of our course project evidence?
