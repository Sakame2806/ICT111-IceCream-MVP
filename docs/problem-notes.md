# Problem Notes

## Selected Case
Campus Digital Art Community Platform (Pixic)

## Target Respondents
| Respondent Type | Why This Respondent Matters | Planned Number | Actual Number |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| Digital Visual Artists | Primary creators who need a space to upload, tag, and organize digital portfolios. | 5 | 5 |
| Digital Art Hobbyists | Consumers who view, like, bookmark, and provide feedback on artwork. | 3 | 3 |
| Art Club Leaders | Organizers who understand current community structures and group pain points. | 2 | 2 |

## Observed / Reported Problem
Student digital visual artists lack a dedicated, organized platform on campus to showcase their work, share ideas, and engage in constructive discussions. Instead, they rely on chaotic chat groups or generic social media networks where their artwork gets buried quickly, and finding peers with similar styles or interests is extremely difficult.

## Current Alternatives or Workarounds
| Workaround | Who Uses It? | Weakness / Pain | Evidence Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Telegram / Line Groups | Student artists & club members | Chat feeds get flooded instantly; images are hard to archive, search, or categorize; feedback is superficial and short-lived. | Interviews (Art Club Members) |
| Instagram / X (Twitter) | Digital illustrators | Algorithmic barriers prevent local discovery; lacks a campus-centric community feel; comments are mostly shallow emojis rather than critiques. | Interview (Freelance Student Artist) |
| Shared Google Drive folders | Collaboration groups | Zero interactive elements (no comments, likes, or bookmarking features); files feel sterile and disconnected. | Observation (Visual Novel Project Group) |

## Repeated Pain Points
| Pain Point | Number of Mentions | Example Evidence | Severity |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Artwork gets buried in chat history | 6 | "I posted my sketch in the Telegram group for feedback, and within 10 minutes it was buried by 50+ lines of text." | High (4/5) |
| Hard to find local collaborators | 4 | "I wanted to find someone who does anime line-art for a game project, but there is no way to search artists by style or tag on campus." | Medium (3/5) |
| Lack of constructive feedback | 5 | "On Instagram, I only get 'looks cool!' or fire emojis. I need actual art critique to improve my coloring and anatomy." | High (4/5) |

## Consequences
| Consequence | Example | Evidence Strength |
|---|---|---|
| Disheartened creators | Several student artists stopped sharing their sketches or progress updates, feeling isolated or ignored in chat rooms. | Strong |
| Inefficient collaboration | Campus project teams (e.g., game dev, event posters) spend weeks trying to find suitable artists through word-of-mouth. | Moderate |

## Notes for MVP Direction
What should the team consider when designing the MVP later?
- Build a responsive web portal (HTML/CSS/JS with Vue3 & Firebase backend) that mimics Pixiv/Pixic structure.
- Focus on portfolio-centric profiles where artists can upload and organize high-res files.
- Implement robust tagging and filtering (e.g., Anime, 3D Render, Concept Art, UI/UX) to make local artists easily searchable.
- Incorporate a dedicated "Critique & Discuss" commenting block under each artwork post to foster active feedback loops.
