# Product Concept - IceCream

## Product Name
IceCream

## Concept Summary
IceCream is a web-based community platform for campus digital artists and hobbyists to showcase their work, discover peers with similar styles or fandom interests, and receive structured critique that helps them improve.

## Problem Statement
Many student and hobbyist artists struggle to share their work in a way that attracts meaningful feedback. Existing alternatives such as Telegram or Line groups often become cluttered, informal, and hard to search. Artwork can disappear quickly, and comments are frequently shallow or unhelpful.

## Target Users
- Campus digital artists
- Illustrators and comic creators
- Hobbyist creators seeking feedback
- Art club members and peers interested in critique culture

## Value Proposition
IceCream gives artists a dedicated space to:
- showcase illustrations, comics, and character sheets
- categorize work by style and fandom
- request specific types of critique
- receive constructive and structured feedback from peers

## Core Product Idea
The platform combines a gallery experience, structured critique forms, and discovery features so that art is easier to find, easier to review, and easier to improve.

## Key Features in the MVP
- Landing page with clear navigation
- Artwork upload form with critique focus selection
- Categorized gallery feed by style and fandom
- Structured critique comments
- Creator dashboard with basic analytics and critique karma

## Success Criteria
The concept is successful if users can:
1. create and upload work to the platform
2. find relevant artwork through categories or search
3. submit meaningful critique rather than simple reactions
4. understand how their work is being reviewed and improved

## Scope Boundary
The first version focuses on community feedback and discovery rather than monetization, commerce, or advanced moderation.
