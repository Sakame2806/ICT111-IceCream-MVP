# Assumption-Evidence Table

| No. | Team Assumption | Evidence Collected | Evidence Type | Strength | Decision |
|---:|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Campus artists are frustrated with general chat groups (Telegram/Line) for showcasing work because messages get buried quickly. | 6/10 interviewees complained about work being lost in active chat feeds within minutes. | Interview | Strong | Keep |
| 2 | Campus artists want a dedicated platform similar to Pixiv/Pixic to tag art, view portfolios, and receive structured feedback. | 8/10 interviewees agreed that having art categories/tags (e.g., character design, 3D model, concept art) would save time and improve discovery. | Interview | Strong | Keep |
| 3 | Artists prefer a web-based portal over a mobile app because of screen size and portfolio organization. | Interviewees prefer web for uploading and detailed reviewing, but want mobile-friendly viewing for casual browsing. | Interview | Moderate | Revise (Focus on responsive web first) |
| 4 | A campus payment system integration for art commissions is immediately required. | Artists prioritized building a local community, showcase gallery, and feedback loop over transaction systems first. | Interview | Moderate | Revise (Defer payment system to a later phase) |


## Evidence Strength Guide
- Weak: team opinion or friend feedback only
- Moderate: repeated interview pattern from target users
- Strong: users show behavior, complete a task, sign up, or provide specific evidence
